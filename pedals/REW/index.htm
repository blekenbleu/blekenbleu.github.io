  • Room Eq Wizard  

    Intro

    REW is not for only rooms [click for details] REW is mostly used to help locate and tune subwoofers,
    mostly by measuring and displaying audio responses.
    Frequency response peaks can be mitigated by equalizing,
    but sharp dips typically result from cancellations AKA destructive interference,
    which are better addressed by relocating a single subwoofer
    or changing phase relations among multiple subwoofers, also possibly by relocation.
    The same techniques will apply for tactile transducers,
    keeping in mind that sound travels faster thru solids than air,
    so that substantial changes in phase relationships are more easily done electronically.

    The Dayton "thruster" transducer audibly rattles at some frequencies if not hand-held against the (30mm MDF) desktop:


    Sensors being used are piezo contact microphone elements epoxied "belly-to-belly",
    with brass discs facing out, shielding both sides. Piezos are wired in parallel,
    effectively lowering source impedance, which is still high enough that,
    fed into a 500kOhm input impedance instrument input,
    cable shielding is very important to avoid AC power hum inductive pickup.
    Wireless mouse RF noise and LCD screen refresh interference may still be audible..

    Thruster movement can be sensed at 12Hz but seems weak until 15Hz, then noticeably stronger above 22Hz.
    Mechanical resonance of the thruster NOT held to 30mm MDF is around 40-45Hz
    Finger-holding a 13mm steel nut against the piezo to approximate mass-loading yields more credible plots:

    .. but results are inconsistent.
    The (consistent) resonance around 15Hz, corresponding to large phase changes, is presumably the 30mm MDF,
    since it corresponds with cancellations. Sweeps while holding the thruster against difference surfaces
    result in resonances at different frequencies. This suggests that, while piezos are uncalibrated and
    probably lack ideally uniform frequency response, they should be able to help analyze differences
    in transducer responses in different configurations.
