pupil swim

Pupil swim is geometric distortion in VR scene features made visible by movements.
Distortions when panning fixed text are quite noticeable,
as in the example @ 0:19/0:41 of this video

Pupil swim becomes evident during Vestibulo-ocular pupil movements
that compensate for head movements.

These effects can be reduced by warping digital scenes to compensate optics aberrations.

Eyebox is the small space where a lens yields best images.
For e.g. microscope and telescope eyepieces,
eyebox is centered at some distance called eye relief.
Outside that space, resolution and sharpness drop while aberrations increase.

Even within a nominal eyebox, abberations can provoke pupil swim
in static scenes for pupils moving relative to headset.

Almalence demonstrates that pupil swim
maintained by blekenbleu
