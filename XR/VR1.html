  • Artur claims [34:14/1:20:13] that main customers will be more consumers than enterprises,
    but IMO cost and value will better fit businesses.
  • VR1 runtime supports masking and mesh upload of CAD for e.g instrument panels,

  • VR1 specification notes: 
    Focus distance < 85cm
Probably prescription lenses from VR Lens Lab, VROpticians
4436 x 4157 is native rendered resolution for 2880x2880 per eye;  35ppd
(same panels as Crystal)
"super clear undistorted 125°+ FoV"
Pimax Crystal QLED vs Somnium VR1;  90 & 120Hz
Mixed reality cameras 2x 4000x3000 with 125°+ FoV
700-850 grams
576 local dimming zones per eye;   perhaps not needed, because low glare optics.
Nofio wireless later.
  • optic block with two aspheric lenses (Artur holds them in the hand)
  • the optic block is 4 times sharper than the panel resolution would require
  • 90 degrees stereo overlap
  • 120+ degrees horizontal FoV
  • local dimming with 576 zones per eye, 200+ nits brightness,
    and while no panel resolution was shared it's obvious it's the same panel as on the Crystal
  • manufacturing tolerance is 10 microns from the lens ideal shape
  • face interface that can be fixed with magnets on the HMD (same mechanism as on HP G2),
    and provided CAD files for people to 3D print their own.
  • one 6m optic cable + something (DisplayPort/VirtualLink/DisplayLink?) for variants without MR cameras,
    and one additional identical cable (connected on the other side of the headset) for MR PC feed.
  • their own OpenXR runtime implementation
  • eye tracking is also their framework implementation,
    but will opensource everything about it, including the algorithm
  • motion compensation is in their framework, and they will expose it for the sim community
  • they also mentioned about how the Mixed Reality cockpit mapping will work:
    you take the CAD of your car/airplane cockpit and feed it to their software,
    and then you can just interact with it with your hands
    (both hand tracking module and MR cameras on the HMD)
  • very large MR camera resolution, something like 4000 x 3000 I think
  • MR camera refresh rate is 90 fps
  • MR angle of view through cameras is 120, just like through the HMD,
    so you will not see the FoV shrink when you enable the MR camera passthrough
  • SteamVR needed for tracking
  • As of 27 Sep 2023, development appears complete;  most remaining issues are financial
  • Sept update and sign-ups likely to slip into first week of Oct.
  • VR2, several years away, will use pancake lenses.
  • 3D video capture from passthru cameras will be a thing;  target for software was unclear (to me).
  • Passthru video processing in the PC, using more CPU resources
    than non-MR/XR HMD of comparable resolution and frame rate
