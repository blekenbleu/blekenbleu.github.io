At Lexmark, I wrote code for Moore-Penrose inverse more than once, having misplaced a first version.  Significantly smaller than others, my algorithm was not embedded in products, being instead used to generate tables that were embedded.  Consequently, filing for a patent was unnecessary, but it now occurs to me that publishing it might have yielded some professional visibility.  Even that second derivation took a week or two, and recreating it now is probably beyond me.  One night recently, I woke up thinking about inflection points while working at IBM and Lexmark.  Before forgetting more of them, here are related anecdotes.
