SC Covid-19 severity by age
South Carolina received a federal disaster declaration for COVID-19 on Mar 28. With a population of over 5.2 million, South Carolina reports testing 3,675 or 0.07%, with 18% positive and most negative tests presumably among health workers. Given general population testing effectively limited to those with serious symptoms, this may be a good population for estimating how serious are symptoms among and between age groups. For example, the percentage of positive tests among those 40-50 is roughly the same as their percentage of SC population. Folks 60-70 appear 73% more likely than them to have severe symptoms while children 10-20 are only 30% as likely.
