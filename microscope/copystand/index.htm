Copy stand camera hanger for macro and micro photography

In the dark ages, when digital cameras were more about convenience than image quality,
I acquired a cheaper but less rigid copy stand than that employed by e.g. Charles Krebs:

The 1.5" o.d. chromed tube by itself flexes enough to require remote shutter release, but its pantograph camera mount and a Manfrotto 454 add too much lost motion for productive macro focus bracketing.
A pair of LAMPHUS Cruizer light bar clamps are being repurposed to rigidly attach a focus bracketing rail.   Clamps include spacers for 1", 1.5" and 1.75" o.d. tubing, but included screws are too short to engage with designated spacers, as Amazon reviewers noted.   Consequently, mismatched 1.5 and 1.75 spacer halves are used.
