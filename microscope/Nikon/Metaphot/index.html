Nikon Metaphot

One of 3 microscopes in Nikon's Microphot V series (pg 68),
along with Biophot and Fluophot, that in introduced Nikon's CF system
with the following characteristics:
  1. Lateral chromatic aberration is corrected properly all the way from field center to edge.
  2. Lack of color changes at eyepiece field stops allows 100% effective field viewing
    (conventional systems have red tinges inside of stops).
  3. No appreciable color changes around eyepiece graticule markings.
  4. Reduced longitudinal chromatic aberration for objective and eyepiece lenses.
  5. Objective and eyepiece lenses are each their own complete optical system
    and can be applied to any field.
Abbe compensation (intentionally introducing sufficient lateral chromatic aberration to balance) was adopted because otherwise correcting lateral chromatic aberration was difficult,
particularly for high magnification objective lenses.
This was resolved by dividing objective lenses into front and rear groups,
over-correcting the front group, then inversely correcting the rear group.

Page 3 of Nikon New CF LENSES clarifies that CF means "chromatic-aberation-free".
Differences among CF/CF N Plan Apochomat, CF/CF N Plan Achromat,
and other CF objectives are on pp 11-13.
Nikon's CF OBJECTIVES 210mm TUBE LENGTH catalog
describes those objections without addressing CF.
